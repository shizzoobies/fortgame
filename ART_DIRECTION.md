# ART_DIRECTION.md

## Goal
Make the game look polished even though all visuals are generated in HTML/CSS/canvas.

## Visual identity
Style target:
- clean fantasy defense
- slightly stylized, readable, friendly
- not gritty realism
- not pixel art unless intentionally chosen consistently
- rich UI panels, glowing accents, layered environment

## Color direction
Use a cohesive palette with:
- cool sky / background tones
- earthy ground tones
- warm gold/orange highlights for coins, projectiles, and important UI
- red/orange hit feedback
- green only for healing or positive health cues

Claude can choose exact colors, but keep strong contrast and readability.

## Background
Suggested background layers:
- sky gradient
- distant mountains or hills
- mid-ground silhouettes
- ground strip / battlefield
- subtle particles or drifting clouds

## Fortress design
Create a fortress on the left side with:
- wall body
- one or two towers
- banner or pennant
- gate or battlement detail
- turret or firing point

It should feel like a fortress rather than a simple rectangle.

## Enemy shapes
Each enemy type should be readable by silhouette:
- Grunt: basic soldier or goblin-like shape
- Runner: leaner, smaller, faster silhouette
- Tank: broad heavy silhouette with shield/armor cues

Use simple shape language and motion differences.

## Combat effects
Use lightweight but satisfying effects:
- projectile trail
- impact sparks
- quick hit flash
- death burst or fade
- damage numbers if possible
- shake or pulse on heavy impact

## UI style
Panels should feel game-like:
- framed cards or stone/metal fantasy panels
- readable buttons
- hover states
- active/inactive button states
- HP bar with clear fill
- gold display that stands out

## Typography
Use web-safe fonts or simple fallback stack only.
Do not rely on external font CDNs.
Make hierarchy clear with sizing, weight, spacing.

## Motion
Use:
- subtle idle movement where possible
- enemy bobbing or gait
- projectile motion arcs or streaks
- panel transitions if lightweight
- game over overlay fade-in

## General quality bar
This should look like a polished prototype, not a wireframe.
